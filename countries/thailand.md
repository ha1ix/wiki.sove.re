# Thailand — Nomad Notes

*Compiled from community digests.*

## Koh Tao

### Diving

Recommended dive operators (as of March 2026): Hydronaughts, Pimp My Dive, Divers Boat, Goodtime, New Haven (Chalok area). March is the start of dive season.

Koh Phangan also has dive spots but less detailed recommendations available.

## Koh Phangan

### Healthcare

Hospital exists on the island but quality reports are mixed. For anything serious, Ko Samui (short ferry) has better facilities. See Ko Samui healthcare section.

### Neighborhoods

Northwest side (Haad Yao, Chalok Lam) regarded as the chill side of the island — better for people wanting quiet beaches and gyms over party scene.

### Notes

Mixed reports on atmosphere. Some friction between locals and tourists reported (scooter near-misses, rudeness). Beaches and nature are the main draw; town areas less so. Best visited with low expectations for nightlife/social scene.

## Chiang Mai

### Air Quality & Burning Season

Burning season runs roughly February–April. As of mid-March 2026: AQI hits red levels in mornings, but conditions varied (some cloudy/rainy days keeping it manageable). Rain has been reported to significantly improve air quality during this period. Air purifiers available in some coworking spaces and hotels — worth confirming before booking. Nomad presence still active during this period despite the air quality.

### Songkran (Thai New Year, mid-April)

Expect chaos. Practical tips: use Grab cars (not scooters), carry dry bags for electronics and documents.

## Pattaya

### Seasonality

Rainy season runs approximately April–November. Rain typically comes in short, intense episodes rather than all-day downpours. Noted as having child-friendly services and infrastructure for families.

## Ko Samui

### Notes

Underrated relative to its Nomad List ranking (as of March 2026). Good beaches, reasonable cost of living, active nightlife. Some power reliability issues reported. Hospital quality on the island is considered decent and significantly cheaper than US equivalents.

### Healthcare

Hospital options across the Gulf islands vary. Ko Samui has the most established facilities; Koh Phangan and Ko Lanta have hospitals but with mixed reports on quality. All considerably cheaper than US healthcare. Worth checking specific hospital reviews before relying on island-based care for anything serious.

## Bangkok

### Food

Healthy eating: Tom Kha Gai and Tom Yum widely available as lower-oil Thai options. Low-oil delivery options exist on Grab but require searching. Finding no-plastic, genuinely low-oil delivery remains difficult.

### Practical

DMK (Don Mueang) airport: night markets located near airport-area hotels — useful for late arrivals or early departures.

Coworking and cafe scene active but no specific venues named in this digest cycle.

### Transit

Bangkok–Surat Thani train route: a practical 12-day itinerary with stops at Sam Roi Yot, Koh Tao, and Koh Phangan before reaching Surat Thani. A good way to see the Gulf coast without flying.

## Phuket

### Sports & Recreation

Tennis academies available for intermediate-level adult players. Kali academy mentioned. Long-stay hotel/villa options near academies. Bangkok also has options (Alpha academy noted for older players).

## Visa & Tax

Free 30-day visa extensions have been reported for travelers affected by Middle East war-related flight cancellations (as of early 2026). Confirm current policy with immigration before relying on this.
